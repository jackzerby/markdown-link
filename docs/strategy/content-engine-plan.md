# Content Engine Plan

## Goal

Run a lightweight but constant content system that turns shipped work into
distribution.

## Core outputs

Every week the marketing team should produce:

- 3 to 5 original X posts
- 10 to 20 reply candidates
- 1 founder memo
- 1 landing page improvement brief
- 1 asset pack for the next feature or launch

## Asset types

- terminal screencasts
- before/after screenshots
- short product GIFs
- quote-card snippets from real markdown docs
- landing page hero copy variants
- pricing and upgrade callout variants

## Landing page workstream

The landing page team should always be testing:

- headline clarity
- command visibility
- social proof placement
- free vs paid explanation
- claim/expiry pain framing
- install CTA versus instant web CTA

## Weekly schedule

### Monday

- CEO picks the weekly story
- launch ops drafts the content calendar
- product strategy selects the most visible feature or proof point
- growth lead reviews adjacent-product and founder research for fresh hooks

### Tuesday

- create screenshots, clips, and markdown examples
- draft 2 original X posts
- propose one landing page change

### Wednesday

- publish one demo post
- send replies to relevant founder and builder threads
- review click and signup response

### Thursday

- publish a founder workflow post
- test a landing page tweak or CTA rewrite
- draft the weekly memo

### Friday

- publish one shipping or proof post
- summarize what got attention
- feed learnings back into next week's plan
- update the research memo with any new onboarding, pricing, or distribution
  patterns the team should study

## Scheduling rule

Always keep:

- 2 posts ready to publish
- 5 replies ready to send
- 1 landing page experiment ready for review
- 1 current internal memo on adjacent-product and founder distribution learnings
