# Adjacent Playbook Research

## Purpose

Give the CEO, launch, growth, and business agents a standing internal memo on
what nearby agent-native products are doing well in onboarding, positioning,
pricing pressure, and founder-led distribution.

This file is for internal strategy only.

## Current adjacent product study

### Product: here.now

What stands out from the public homepage:

- the promise is immediate and narrow: free, instant web hosting for agents
- the homepage explains the flow in two steps instead of leading with a feature grid
- it removes account friction by allowing anonymous publishing first
- it highlights broad agent compatibility right away
- it uses expiry and claim behavior to create a natural reason to sign up

What stands out from the published skill and docs:

- the product is designed around agent execution, not just human dashboard use
- the install path is explicit and copyable for agent tools
- the API and scripting surface are part of the product story, not hidden
- the free-vs-authenticated differences are easy to understand
- handles, links, claim codes, and custom domains extend the product after the
  first successful publish instead of blocking it

Lessons for markdown.link:

- keep the top-level promise painfully specific
- show the exact command and the resulting URL as early as possible
- let users taste the product before making them do account work
- make claim and paid upgrade flows feel like a continuation of success, not a
  detour
- keep docs, scripts, and agent instructions unusually polished because they
  are part of the actual acquisition funnel

Where markdown.link should differ:

- stay even narrower around markdown, memos, specs, plans, and founder docs
- lean harder into rendered versus raw markdown views as a core artifact feature
- make the product feel calmer and more minimal than a generic static host
- price around permanent markdown sharing and control, not broad website hosting

## Founder distribution study

### Founder: Adam Ludwin

What we could verify directly:

- the product has strong public packaging for agent-native installation and use
- the public materials emphasize speed, compatibility, and immediate utility
- the skill/docs surface is crisp enough that distribution and product are
  reinforcing each other

What appears to be happening around the founder motion:

- founder-led distribution is likely tightly coupled to product behavior, not
  brand campaigns
- the product seems to win by making a technical workflow easy to show in one
  short clip or one short post
- the public operating style appears to favor simple proof over heavy narrative

How to study this responsibly:

- review public landing page and docs weekly
- track what new onboarding or packaging changes appear
- review public founder posts when accessible and log repeated themes, hooks,
  and product demos
- do not copy public language verbatim; extract the mechanic behind it

## What the marketing team should do every week

- capture 3 to 5 learnings from adjacent product onboarding or founder posts
- turn each learning into either a landing-page test, a post draft, or a
  pricing/packaging question
- record which learnings actually improved clicks, installs, publishes, or
  upgrades
- delete stale observations that are no longer helping the company make better
  decisions

## Active hypotheses for markdown.link

- the best converting top-of-funnel message will stay command-first
- founder and operator content will outperform broad "AI productivity" content
- expiry pressure plus one clean paid tier can produce a simple upgrade story
- the landing page should feel more like a product instruction than a brochure
- marketing assets should look like real working notes, not designed ads
