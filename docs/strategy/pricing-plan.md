# Pricing Plan

## Recommendation

Start with one paid tier at `$5/month`.

## Why `$5/month` fits

- low enough for solo founders and indie hackers
- high enough to create a real upgrade decision
- simple enough to explain in one line on the landing page
- aligned with the narrow value proposition

## Free plan

The free plan should prove the core loop:

- publish markdown instantly
- share the link
- feel the expiry limit when the content matters

## Paid plan

The paid plan should remove friction, not add complexity.

Suggested paid benefits:

- permanent links
- custom domains
- higher usage limits
- more reliable agent and API-key workflows

## Pricing page message

Free:

- great for quick shares
- links expire

Paid:

- keep important links live
- share on your own domain
- use markdown.link as part of your daily workflow

## What to avoid

- multiple paid tiers at launch
- enterprise contact forms too early
- pricing based on abstract credits
- hiding the main paid benefit behind too many feature bullets

## When to revisit pricing

Revisit after:

- repeated free-to-paid demand signals
- real usage data on publish volume and storage
- evidence that a team plan is needed
